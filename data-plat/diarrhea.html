  • Symptoms

    • Sudden vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain and cramps, nausea; less common symptoms include headache, low-grade fever, muscle aches, chills, and fatigue

  • How Common

    • About 23 million people are infected with noroviruses in the U.S. each year, resulting in 50,000 hospitalizations.

  • Overview

    • Noroviruses are a group of viruses that cause inflammation of the stomach and intestines. Severe diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach pain are the most common symptoms. Most people know norovirus infection as "stomach flu," although it's not caused by a flu virus. Noroviruses are contagious and often cause outbreaks among people who live, work, or eat in close quarters. That's because they are easily spread through contaminated food or water, by touching a contaminated surface, or from person to person. There's no specific treatment for noroviruses, although it's important to get enough fluids. Infants, older adults, and people with chronic illnesses are at higher risk for dehydration from noroviruses and may need medical care.
