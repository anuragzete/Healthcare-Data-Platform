  • Symptoms

    • Symptoms of deep vein blood clots in the leg may include swelling in the leg or along the leg vein, pain or tenderness, which may only happen when standing or walking, warmth near the swelling or pain, and red or discolored skin. Symptoms of pulmonary embolism may include shortness of breath, rapid breathing, pain when breathing, coughing up blood, and fast heart rate.

  • How Common

    • Almost one million people – most older than age 40 -- develop deep vein thrombosis each year in the U.S.

  • Overview

    • Blood clots in the leg form when blood thickens and clumps in a thigh or calf vein. But a clot deep in the leg, especially the thigh, can break off, travel to the lungs, and block blood flow – a condition called pulmonary embolism. This can damage the lungs and other organs and cause death. Sitting for a long time or bed rest, surgery, or lower body injury, such as a broken hip, can cause a blood clot in the leg. You may feel leg pain or have no symptoms. Anticoagulants, or blood thinners, can help stop the blood clot from forming or getting bigger.
